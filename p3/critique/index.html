Portfolio Website Review

Case study of five portfolio websites in preparation for project two.

Jump to section:

Heather Shaw | Mingfei Yang | Kate Moross | Tobias van Schneider | Brook Perryman

Heather Shaw

Visit Website

Heather's website makes use of bright colors and interactive design to engage the user. The website design is simple with no navigation, other than highlighting her work. After scrolling through her work, she has a simple button for contact, and also an about me page and links to her social media. She highlights various creative work and brings focus with her sharp, clean site.


Mingfei Yang

Visit Website

Mingfei highlights the games he has worked on, and has a simple navigation. He highlights his professional work and personal work separately, and uses his about page to write about himself, but also provide a streamlined resume. With a page designated to contact and an embedded form, he makes it easy to navigate, learn more about himself and his works, and get in touch.


Kate Moross

Visit Website

Kate's website is playful and fun, slapping you in the face with color right up front. By contrast, her logo design is a simple, streamlined, single font of her name which helps to bring focus to her works. Kate breaks down her work by type, including pages for Hand Drawn Type, Geometric, Editorial, and Collaborations. I enjoy that she calls her "about me" section "Biography" but also think it led me to believe it would be a bit more than the single paragraph she included there.


Tobias van Schneider

Visit Website

Tobias' home page is playful, easy to read, and different than the typical graphical grid that often portfolios use to lay out their designs. His page is designed to scroll and move vertically, which I think makes it seamless, but also gives him control of what shows in what order so he can highlight what he considers to be his most successful projects. However, the tradeoff for this, is that if I as the viewer was interested in a specific project or type of project, there's not a good index at a glance that lets me choose what to look at.


Brook Perryman

Visit Website

Brook organizes her portfolio according to media, and uses the hover interaction to convey those groups. Her contact/resume page embeds her resume directly in the browser while also providing a PDF download. I wish she also included a bio or paragraph about herself and a photo--even when writing this paragraph, I had to deduce from the name of her instagram that she was female. She does put her social media and contact information front and center and keeps the experience streamlined and quick. Since she has email on her contact page, I think she should also include an icon in her left navigation menu as well.
